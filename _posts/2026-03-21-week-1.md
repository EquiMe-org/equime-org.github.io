## Gratitude

I'm grateful for the people beside me building this company. We resourced effectively, and win or lose we're doing this the right way.

## This Week's Focus

Financial projections. We needed a model we could put in front of investors that tells a coherent story about where this business goes.

The first iteration was terrible. But then I remembered a line from the folks who wrote *Venture Deals*:

> "The only thing that we know about financial predictions of startups is that 100 percent of them are wrong."

That reframed things. The projections don't need to be precisely correct. They need to be directionally correct. They need to show the train of thought: how we think about revenue, what levers we believe matter, what the economics look like at scale. Directionally correct is better than no answer at all.

We also started planning the beta in earnest. We're targeting a live launch this summer with roughly 30 users. There are some technical architecture decisions that need to be locked down before we get there.

## Wins

We brought on another founder this week. He brings creativity and business structure to the platform, and he can hit the ground running from a technical perspective. The team is getting stronger. The pace of decision-making is accelerating because of it.

Having someone who can help shape the idea and plan for its execution, not just build what's specced, is a different kind of value. It changes the conversations you have.

## Challenges / What Broke

The frustration this week is the unglamorous reality of pre-launch: planning before coding.

We've built a handful of different MVPs and proof-of-concepts over the past year, each testing different economics and implementations of the core idea. But to get to the point where we can confidently start writing beta-launch code, there's a lot of planning and iteration that has to happen first. Architecture decisions. Token economics. Compliance frameworks. User flows.

It takes time. When you're wired to want to ship, the planning phase feels like friction. Even though you know it's the thing that separates a product that works from one that collapses under its own weight.

Start mapping the beta user pipeline: who are the first 30, and how do we onboard them in a way that gives us real signal on what works and what doesn't.
