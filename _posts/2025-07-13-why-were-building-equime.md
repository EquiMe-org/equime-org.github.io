I wanted to play fantasy baseball on a platform that leveraged player values like a stock. Buy low on a rookie, sell high after a breakout season. Simple concept. But when I went looking for it, there was no stock. No market. No mechanism to make it happen.

Other companies have tried starting here and failed. They built the fantasy layer, the gamified wrapper, the social features. But they all needed the underlying market to exist first. You can't build the toys without building the exchange.

We iterated on the idea and realized it wasn't tied to baseball or fantasy at all. There was a deeper issue we could solve. There is no clean, fair mechanism for people with potential to raise capital from the people who believe in them.

## Beyond Baseball

Think about the creator who got their rights stripped by a music label. The startup founder who needed capital for infrastructure but didn't want to give up control or take on predatory debt. The kid who tried GoFundMe to afford hockey skates. These are all versions of the same problem.

The financial institutions that exist today don't care about the person or the product. They care about the return. The people who partner with those institutions often give up control, take on debt they can't service, and lose sight of the thing that put a fire in their bones in the first place.

equiMe is built to fix that.

## What equiMe Actually Is

equiMe is a personal asset platform. We enable individuals (athletes, creators, entrepreneurs, anyone with potential) to issue personal assets that represent a stake in their future brand value. Investors buy those assets. As the person succeeds, demand drives the asset value higher. Early believers participate in the upside.

Think of it as stock in people, not companies.

We're built on the Avalanche blockchain. Institutional-grade infrastructure with sub-second finality and low transaction costs. There's a primary sale (like an IPO, but for a person) and a secondary market where assets trade freely based on supply and demand.

The one-sentence version: **you can invest directly in someone you believe in.**

When I say that to people, ears perk up. They start asking questions about how the asset works, what the creator gets, how investors trade. Usually after a minute they're excited and want to hear more. The goal is to eventually not need the minute. Get the idea across in one sentence and have it click.

## The Headwinds We Know About

I'm not going to pretend this is easy. There are two major headwinds we think about constantly.

**Credibility.** The blockchain space has a stigma problem, and it's earned. The technology is genuinely revolutionary, but it's been misapplied and weaponized for most of its existence. We need everyone who interacts with equiMe to see us as a credible exchange. A place you can park investments and not worry about security, ethics, or fraud. That trust has to be built deliberately, over time, through execution and transparency. It's part of why I'm writing this blog.

**Complexity.** This is a layered idea. Personal asset issuance sits at the intersection of blockchain, securities law, creator economics, and traditional finance. Getting all of that to feel simple and intuitive to a first-time user is a design and communication challenge we haven't fully solved yet.

## Why Now

Three things are converging.

The NIL market created a billion-dollar opportunity for collegiate athletes overnight. The creator economy is approaching $500 billion. And blockchain infrastructure has finally matured to the point where you can build real, scalable financial products on it without the UX feeling like a science experiment.

Meanwhile, AI is automating content at an accelerating rate. When anyone can generate anything, the value of real human potential (authenticity, skill, reputation) skyrockets. Human authenticity is the new alpha.

## Why Us

We founded equiMe about a year ago, though the idea has been years in the making. I still haven't quit my day job. I'm an IAM engineer at Koch Industries, and I take my responsibilities there seriously. But the closer we get to a funding round, the closer that changes. I want to work full time on something that has this many positive impacts on people's lives.

We're pre-revenue, pre-seed, and building. The MVP is in active development. The legal infrastructure is in place. The financial models are built. Every week we're getting closer.

This blog is the record of that process. Not the polished version. The real one.
